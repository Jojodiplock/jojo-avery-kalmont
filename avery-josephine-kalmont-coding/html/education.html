Hypothermia Safety

Hypothermia happens when your body loses heat faster than it can produce it, causing your core temperature to drop dangerously low. It's important to recognize the early signs like shivering, confusion, and slurred speech. Learning hypothermia safety teaches you how to dress properly in cold weather, stay dry, and seek shelter when needed. It also shows you how to help someone with hypothermia by warming them slowly and getting medical help. Understanding hypothermia can help you stay safe and protect others in cold environments.

Emergency Preparedness Guide

An Emergency Preparedness Guide helps you get ready for unexpected situations like earthquakes, floods, power outages, or other disasters. It teaches you how to make a plan, build an emergency kit with food, water, and supplies, and stay informed through alerts. You also learn how to safely evacuate or shelter in place, and how to help others. Being prepared gives you confidence and keeps you and your loved ones safer during emergencies.
